What Does Estate Planning Entail?

Will Formation

Call Bespoke Estate law for help writing a will that is clear, legally compliant within the state of South Carolina, and written, signed, and witnessed by at least two individuals. Don’t leave the future of your estate to chance or the whims of legal challenges. This document will include where your assets are distributed, and it can even appoint a guardian for your minor children.

Trust Formation

South Carolina residents may choose from many types of trusts, depending on their needs. These include special needs trusts, marital trusts, charitable trusts and more. You have many tools at your disposal that will help your protect your assets and distribute them responsibility, and we are here to help you choose the right trust or other vehicle for your estate.

Elder Law

This aspect of estate planning focuses on the needs of older adults, addressing issues such as Medicare planning, elder abuse, and competency concerns. It guarantees that seniors have their legal, financial, and health needs appropriately managed as they age. We have helped many clients plan for their own future as they age, and we can provide caring and insightful planning services to you that are rooted in our experience.

Avoiding Probate

Strategies like the creation of revocable living trusts, naming beneficiaries on retirement and bank accounts, or establishing joint ownership can help bypass the probate process. Don’t leave your heirs fighting amongst themselves – call us for a free consultation regarding your estate today.

Caring For Your Loved Ones After Worst-Case Scenarios

It’s your responsibility to establish legal frameworks, such as conservatorships and guardianships, to protect your loved ones in case of a disaster. You can also call us regarding the creation of powers of attorney and HIPAA authorizations to handle financial and health decisions, respectively. Also consider health care proxies to make medical decisions, and living wills to express wishes regarding end-of-life care and organ donation.

Business Succession Planning

If you’re planning to retire and leave your business to certain leadership within your team, we would be happy to speak with you about responsible succession planning. You want to make sure the business is left in good hands, continues to provide jobs for valuable employees, and has defense mechanisms against irresponsible handling of funds.
